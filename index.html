Rainbow Square

Snap Square

I created a snap project with a variety of different variables to make a series or squares in a certain perspective. Our goal was to just make a block to draw a square and to be able to change the side length very easily and we did it by making loops but I decided to take it a step further and I wanted to make random sized squares. The challenging part was putting it into perspective but I figured out that if you do a little math to distance the square based on its random side length you could then have the random square make themself into perspective.

Cat and Rat Game

Snap link

In this game we learned how to actually code a game with interacting variables. The most challenging part was just that, to have our different sprites and backgrounds interact with one another's movements and to change bases on what you did as a player. The controls were easy but changing scenes and having moving obstacles was harder to make more solid. We used a lot of “if touching this color” blocks to activate most of our variables. It brought a lot of insight to us through making it though, the logic we practiced in making it was super helpful to what we are learning now.
